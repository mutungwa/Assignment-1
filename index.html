Donald J. Trump

45th President of the United States Businessman Philanthropist

Biography

Donald John Trump, born on June 14, 1946, is an American businessman, media personality, and politician who served as the 45th president of the United States from 2017 to 2021. Before entering politics, he was a successful real estate developer and television host. His leadership style and policies have had a significant impact on the United States and the world.

Major Achievements

  • Signed the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, reducing corporate and individual tax rates.
  • Revamped trade agreements, including the USMCA (United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement).
  • Brokered historic peace deals in the Middle East, including the Abraham Accords.
  • Strengthened the U.S. military and increased defense spending.
  • Led economic growth and record-low unemployment before the COVID-19 pandemic.

Legacy

Donald Trump's presidency was marked by bold decisions, economic policies, and foreign relations strategies that continue to influence global politics. His role as a businessman and media personality, along with his impact on conservative movements, makes him one of the most influential figures in modern American history.
